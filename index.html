Hair–Accessory Perceptual Study

This is a short perceptual study on 3D rendering of hair and head accessories. You will watch a series of brief video clips showing an animated head wearing a hat, and answer simple questions about each.

  • The study takes about 10 minutes.
  • No personal or identifying information is collected. Responses are stored anonymously and used solely for thesis research.
  • You may stop at any time by closing the tab. Partial data is discarded.
  • Please complete the study on a desktop or laptop with sound on and a stable internet connection.

Instructions

You will see two types of trials:

Comparison trials

Two video clips are shown side by side. Both show the same head wearing the same hat, but rendered with slightly different settings. Pick the clip that looks more like a real person naturally wearing the hat. There are no right or wrong answers — trust your first impression.

Ranking trials

Three video clips are shown together, labelled A, B, and C. Rank them by how much the hat appears to be pressing the hair down. Use the dropdown under each clip to assign a rank (1 = least pressed, 3 = most pressed). Each rank must be used exactly once.

Trials are presented in a random order. Each clip is 5 seconds and loops automatically while you decide.

Three short questions (optional)

These help us check for sampling effects in the data. Skip any you'd rather not answer.
