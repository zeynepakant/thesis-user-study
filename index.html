Hair–Accessory Perceptual Study

This is a short perceptual study on 3D rendering of hair and head accessories. You will watch a series of brief video clips showing an animated head wearing a hat, and answer simple questions about each.

  • The study takes about 10 minutes.
  • No personal or identifying information is collected. Responses are stored anonymously and used solely for thesis research.
  • You may stop at any time by closing the tab. Partial data is discarded.
  • Please complete the study on a desktop or laptop with a stable internet connection.

Instructions

You will see two types of trials:

Note on the stimuli. The clips show a simplified 3D rendering of a knit beanie sitting on an animated head. The beanie shape is a geometric representation rather than a photoreal asset — we are studying the deformation behaviour, not the photographic look. Please rate based on whether the hat appears to sit on the hair the way a real beanie would, not on how textured or detailed the render is.

Comparison trials

Two video clips are shown side by side. Both show the same head wearing the same hat, but rendered with slightly different settings. Pick the clip that looks more like a real person naturally wearing the hat. Then rate the clip you chose on a 1–7 scale (1 = artificial, 7 = real person). There are no right or wrong answers — trust your first impression.

Ranking trials

Three video clips are shown together, labelled A, B, and C. Rank them by how much the hat appears to be pressing the hair down. Use the dropdown under each clip to assign a rank (1 = least pressed, 3 = most pressed). Each rank must be used exactly once.

Trials are presented in a random order. Each clip is 5 seconds and loops automatically while you decide. At the end you will see a short qualitative section showing the same pipeline applied to a more realistic beanie mesh, followed by three more ranking trials on that realistic mesh.

One more section: realistic-asset examples

The clips you saw in the main trials used a simplified geometric beanie. The clips below apply the same pipeline to a realistic, artist-authored beanie mesh — closer to how the system would look in a finished avatar application.

Please rate each clip on the same 1–7 plausibility scale.

Last section: realistic-beanie ranking

Three more ranking trials using the same realistic beanie mesh. The task is identical to the main ranking trials: rank the three clips by how much the hat appears to be pressing the hair down.
